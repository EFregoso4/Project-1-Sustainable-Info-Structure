Planning Your Research

Start by defining clear research questions and identifying reliable sources. Organizing your approach before collecting data saves time and ensures accuracy.

Tip: Use library databases and academic journals for credible information. Make a list of key sources to guide your data collection and analysis.

Formatting Your Research Data

Before analyzing, your data should be well-organized and clearly labeled:

  • Assign unique, intuitive names to each variable.
  • Use consistent formatting for categorical and continuous variables.
  • Separate multi-component columns into individual variables (e.g., tasks into task1, task2, task3).
  • Check for missing values and correct errors.
  • Dichotomize categorical variables when appropriate (0 = no, 1 = yes).

Proper formatting ensures that your Excel spreadsheets and statistical software will process your data correctly.

Preparing for Analysis

Once your dataset is organized:

  • Create a separate sheet for formatted data.
  • Double-check variable names, value coding, and column order.
  • Ensure continuous variables are numeric and categorical variables are coded correctly.
  • Keep a master reference list of all variables for reproducibility.

After completing these steps, you will be ready to move on to the next module for hands-on data analysis (Data Analysis Module).
